General Information

Overwatch is a free-to-play First Person Shooter (FPS) co-op game. Launched in 2016, the game was created by Blizzard entertainment and is available on PC, Playstation, Xbox, and Nintendo Switch.

The servers for Overwatch 1 shut down on October 3rd, 2022. It later relaunched as Overwatch 2 in 2023, however most of the core mechanics are the same (with slight alterations). For example, both Overwatch1 & 2 have you engaging in player vs player combat on a team; however, Overwatch 2 has decreased the team count from 6 players to 5 per team.

Some core mechanics of the game this field guide goes over include:

Heroes, combat mechanics, gamemodes, events, and others.

Heroes

The game's most popular feature is their roster of playable characters in the game also known as their Heroes. As of 2026, there are currently 51 heroes total; 32 from Overwatch 1 and 19 additional heroes since Overwatch 2 launched.

There are 3 primary classes of heroes, and each class has 3-4 subclasses, each giving the player a bonus passive.

  • Tanks:
    Your main damage takers; they should be leading the charge for the team.
    • Buisers: Heroes like , Roadhog, and Zarya gain resistance to critical hits and move faster while below half health.
    • Initiators: Heroes like D.VA, Doomfist, and Winston heal overtime while staying airborne.
    • Stalwarts: Heroes like Junker Queen, Rammatra, and Reinhardt gain resistance to knockback and being slowed.
  • DPS or Damage:
    Your main damage dealer; they seek out, engage, and eliminate the enemy.
    • Flankers: Heroes like Genji, Reaper, and Tracer allow health packs on you find on the map restore more health
    • Recons: Heroes like Echo, Sombra, and Pharah reveal enemies below half health
    • Sharpshooters: Heroes like Ashe, Hanzo, and Widowmaker cause critical hits to reduce your movement ability cooldown.
    • Specialists: Heroes like Bastion, Mei, and Soldier: 76 increase your reload speed upon eliminating an enemy.
  • Support:
    Your main healers, shield-providers, and damage boosters; if your support survives, your team does too.
    • Medics: Heroes like Mercy, Moira, and Kiriko allow healing with your weapon to heal yourself
    • Survivors: Heroes like Brigitte, Illari, and Juno cause your movement abilities to start passive health regeneration.
    • Tactitions: Heroes like Ana, Baptiste, and Zenyatta store excess ultimate charge.

Combat

While every hero has their own unique playstyle and abilities, they share the same three core mechanics: their main weapon, skills, and ultimate. These mechanics also have different types of effects depending on the hero

Mechanics
  • Main Weapon: This is what the player attack with regardless of which hero you pick. That being said, not all heroes have the same type of main weapon.
  • Skills: These are the secondary abilities from your weapon and each hero has at least 2; skills do not need to be reloaded, but they do have a cooldown.
  • Ultimate: Also known as "ultimate ability", it is a special mechanism that's charged to 100% over the course of the game. Players charge their ultimate through combat, tracking progress via a meter that you and your team can see.
Effects

Most effects are exclusive to wepaons or skills/ultimates. The two exceptions to this are hitscans and projectiles.

  • Skills/Ultimates:
    • Area of Effect (Aoe): abilities that affect all heroes in their area & often require an unobstructed line of sight.
    • Hitscans: ammo without travel time between firing and hiting the target.
    • Projectiles: ammo with a size & travel time that deals damage to any target it collides with.
    • Targeted: abilities that target a specific enemy.
    • Movement: abilities that allow the player extra movement through leaping, dashing, teleporting or increasing speed overall.
  • Weapons:
    • Shotguns: weapons that fire a large number of ammo and damage; can be either hitscan or projectile.
    • Beams: weapons that are channeled instead of fired.
    • Meelee: short ranged weapons that don't worry about ammo or reload time.

Events

Overwatch adds a variety of events for players, paired with rewards and in-game map decorations, usually based around the holidays.

A non-extensive list of said events include:
  1. Lunar New Year: based off the real Lunar New Year where each event is based off the chinese zodiac of that year (ex. 2026 was year of the horse.)
  2. Pride: occurs every June meant to celebrate both the LGBT+ heroes and players within the community.
  3. Summer Games: occurs during one month in the summer, features the event exclusive game "Lucioball," a futball based game with brazillian character Lucio
  4. Halloween Terror: occurs during Halloween and includes event exclusive games such as "Junkerstein's Laboratory," "Junkerstein's Revenge," and "Wrath of the Bride".
  5. Winter Wonderland: occurs from December to January and includes event exclusive games such as "Mei's Snowball Offensive" and "Yeti Hunt".

Other/Misc.

As Overwatch has gained popularity across both games for 10 years, this section is dedicated to additional beloved features of the game.

Cosmetics
  • Hero skins: The most popular cosmetic, they're costumes designed for a character and their weapon. While most skins can be bought in the shop, they range from rare, epic, legendary (hard to come by), or mythic (exclusive customizable skins only available via the battlepass).
  • Sprays: grafitti or stickers players can put on the map (they do dissapear when times out, a player leaves, or when reapplying).
  • Profile: players can customize their profile with a variety of profile pictures and namecards to choose from.
  • Emotes: animated gestures for players in game.
  • Highlight intros: brief animated intros when a player gets "play of the game."
Battle Pass

Introduced in Overwatch 2, the battle pass is a monetization system where players can earn points to win various cosmetics. Some cosmetics are exclusive to the BP while some can be bought in the shop or players can buy extra battle pass points to get further rewards.

Shop

As previously mentioned, the shop is a feature where players can by cosmetics with either coins or credits; both can be earned through the battle pass, but you can also buy coins with real currency. The shop has a "featured" section with popular cosmetics or a "for you" section more curated to your tastes.
