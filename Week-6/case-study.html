Case Study

Overwatch Field Guide

Design Choices

What design choices did you make & why?

One of the things I really appreciate about Overwatch from a design perspective is how it has a simple yet iconic color palette. You have your cool neutrals while keeping the orange has a striking accent colors. Additionally, since my field guide was inspired by the UIX design of the settings, it also included a striking teal for interactive items (ie navigation). The one thing I did struggle with (slightly) was the choice of typography as I couldn't find the font Overwatch uses (Kenyan Coffee according to online forums) as a google font. Luckily, I was able to work around this; understanding a recurring motif in Overwatch's overall design are sci-fi/technology aesthetics, I was able to find Science Gothic for my section headers and Mozilla Text for the navigation text.

One thing I truly wanted to emulate was their navigation system, very spaced out wherein it would highlight teal when interacting with it. The navigation system and the gradient background for the page overall are what I took directly from the game design wise. As for the sections, I wanted to keep it very simple with just a blue-grey margin strip at the top for an accent color. In the end, I didn't quite know where to place the orange, so I decidd to use it for the header at the top of the page.

Technical Challenges

What was difficult to code? How did you solve it?

The most difficult part has to be the navigation system, primarily the css aspects. I was struggling with making sure each section was spaced out like it's inspiration but I couldn't quite figure that out. Luckily, I had w3schools to cross reference & figured out how to ensure each list item had it's own individual background without any list styling. I did have to use copilot to refresh how to make the navigation sticky as I couldn't readily find it on google (I just needed align-self). I was also tinkering with the aesthetics of the footer (before I saw I accidentally put it in the main tag, then I put it outside and it worked)

Most of the coding was fairly managable with plenty of references on standby, but (this probably doesn't answer the question) my primary struggle was actually making sure I got all the content of the game within the field guide. Because of how extensive the gameplay mechanics are, I wanted to make sure I had enough to get all the necessary details without making it seem overwhelming/exhausting.

Responsive Strategy

How did you adapt your design for mobile

It was...interesting. The section designs were easy, but because Overwatch is already built for a wide screen (PC, TV, Switch, etc), I wasn't quite sure how to navigate (pun intended) the design for the navigation system. Ultimately, I decided on something a simple hamburger menu for mobile (which was also very fitting) and to instead have a full background for the menu rather than each item being separated like on desktop.

Learning Moments

What did you learn about Flexbox or CSS positioning

I learned (and loved) how to figure out sticky navigation (it's just fun).

Future Improvements

What would you add or change with more time?

I would instead change the section headers to something more stylistic like the namecards mentioned in cosmetics.
