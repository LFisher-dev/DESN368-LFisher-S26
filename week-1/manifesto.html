The Manifesto of the Fisher

Lee Fisher's manifesto for academic success while taking code and web design. These will also be your key tips for maintaining success that we've* established do and will work FOR YOU

We is referring to Lee (typing) and Lee (reading in the future), which for the sake of this, are two separate entities.

Have Confidence In Your Capabilities

we both know exactly what's going on here. You have worked to get here. Your teachers see it, your peers see it, your family sees it. You've also put a lot of money into this but It Has Been Worth It. you've learned throughout the quarters and it shows. However, we both know that for you: knowing is different than seeing it. Learn to SEE you're capable.

get better at time management

set aside frequent study time so that way you don't feel like you're scrambling to get things done. AND (because of recent situations), learn to break things down and then take a break (for a certain period of time) after going A Thing; stopping in the middle of a thing without knowing when to jump back in is a doozy.

keep up with assignments and when they're posted

Be sure to be aware of when an assignment is due. Importantly, be aware of how long it will take you (not how long generally, for YOU) to do said assignment. You may think it will take shorter than expected, but when you spend that time daydreaming about your favorite characters kissing, you will check in at 11:50 and see you only have ten minutes (DONT DO THAT).

don't be afraid to ask for clarification or help if needed

I can smell that elementry school perfectionist anxiety on you (or maybe that's the brown sugar boba shampoo). If there's something you're struggling with (whether you're having trouble understanding or doing something) DO! NOT! BE! AFRAID! TO! ASK!!! Instructors are there to Instruct and to Help; they may give you feedback, but that's different from giving you judgement.

don't die (/j)

But seriously, don't be like icarus; learn to know when you're too close to the sun and come back down before you burn. Once you're following your game plan, don't be afraid to take those breaks during assignments. Even if you need a day to recuperate, just be sure to communicate that and have a game plan to catch up!! do NOT make yourself SICK for an A.
