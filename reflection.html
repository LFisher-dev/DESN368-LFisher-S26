My Reflection

"The Threshold" What was the specific moment this quarter where the code "clicked" for you?"

Before I give my answer, I want to note that I came into the class with some basic understanding of HTML and CSS (shoutout to my Web Design class in freshman year of high school and Archive of Our Own). That being said, I'd consider my threshold moment to be when we started out Field Guide project. The reason I say so is because that's when we started getting into responsive design, something that went beyond the basic knowledge I had. I also highlight this project in particular because it also widened my understanding of how code can operate and work, thus furthering how to go about the design process.

"The Pit: What was the hardest assignment? How did you get yourself out of the "pit of despair"?"

The hardest assignment has to be a tie between the landing page and, ironically, the field guide. I give that title to one of them not because of what was asked, but rather how vast they felt in terms of what I needed to do. However, what got me out of this pit was breaking it down into lists of what I needed to do, then making notes of what I could still do/change. Being able to check off each part and then look at the progress I make through the assignment really boosted my confidence in being able to get them done.

"The Pivot: Look at your original "Moodz and Vibes." How does your final portfolio compare? Did you stick to the plan or pivot? Why?"

If a pivot is any change to the design at any capacity, then yes I did pivot regarding my typography & color palette. However, I'd still consider it a soft pivot because I stuck true to the more "whimsical" aspects of my moodboard. The reason I pivoted in the first place is because I wanted to go for something that felt more presentable withinin a professional context (i.e., more "subdued" fonts and a monochromatic adjacent palette). Plus, it helped that I could find something of a similar vibe that I could get off google fonts.

"The Process: What would you change about how you approached this course? (e.g., time management, reading documentation, asking for help)."

The biggest thing I would change is what I call my "sticking point," which is what I describe the point to where I can start a project. This does play into time management and asking for help as I have a bad habit of either putting things off to the last minute and stressing myself out the night before a deadline, or I overthink my ability to put out a good product and then never follow through on the assignment in the end. As mentioned in "the pit" question, a continuous checklist has been a big step in changing the time management portion of this habit. That being said, I'd change how I view needing help; when I was starting to struggle with work, I should've taken that as a sign to ask for some guidance rather than just shut everything out and try to figure it out on my own.

"The Future: How do you see this hybrid skillset (Design + Code) impacting your future practice?"

As mentioned in "the threshold" answer, learning about things like responsive design and page layout has helped me change my idea of design expectations, especially when tackling web design. From the design angle, I've gained a good grasp on how to put an idea down to base my code off of; from the code angle, I now have an understanding of what it takes to put said design together and how to adapt when something needs to be changed while sticking to the original design as soon as possible.. I have to remember what can work on a website while also being able to experiment with that does work.
